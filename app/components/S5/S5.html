Evaluating and improving your wellness through time out

  • Counterbalance your pursuits outside of work with your work pursuits and engage in activities that are in direct contrast in terms of style and activity.
  • If your job involves you sitting down and doing repetitive and monotonous tasks try to compensate by selecting recreational pursuits that are more varied and energetic in character and vice versa.
  • If you do your job in a highly competitive environment and feel driven to succeed, try to compensate by doing less time/pressure orientated and more co-operative sporting/leisure activities to redress the balance.
  • If you are a compulsive individual, who feels obliged to engage in ‘any activity’ when not working or you are plagued by feelings of guilt and anger, then take time out and simply do nothing. You may want to try yoga and/or meditation-like exercise to help you here.
  • See what it feels like to relax and not to have to do anything at all. If you are not having problems with sleeping at night, you could try adopting the habit of taking a short ‘power nap’ during the midday for about 15 to 20 minutes.
  • Gauge your sense of wellness when you say confidently to someone:
    1. I am busy relaxing”. It may sound awkward to you at first explaining why you are doing nothing but it should be worth the effort.
  • Relaxation is itself a recreational activity, and is probably one of the most complex and difficult to learn
